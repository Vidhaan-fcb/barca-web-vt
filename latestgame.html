Overview

On July 31, 2025, FC Barcelona played FC Seoul in an exciting pre-season friendly at the Seoul World Cup Stadium, marking the club's return to South Korea after more than 15 years. The match was part of Barça’s Asian summer tour and ended in an emphatic 7–3 win for the Catalan giants, showcasing their attacking firepower and deep squad. Barcelona started with a strong lineup that included Lewandowski, Pedri, Raphinha, and 17-year-old sensation Lamine Yamal, who wore the iconic No. 10 shirt and delivered a brilliant performance. Yamal scored twice in the first half, one of which came after a stunning dribble and finish, while Lewandowski opened the scoring by tapping in a rebound. Although FC Seoul—currently fourth in the K League and featuring veteran Jesse Lingard—responded with two goals, one ruled out for offside, they couldn’t maintain control for long. The first half ended 3–2 in favor of Barcelona, but the real story unfolded after halftime when coach Hansi Flick substituted all eleven players, bringing in younger talents and bench players. The second half saw Barcelona’s depth take over. Andreas Christensen blasted in a long-range rocket, Gavi scored with a composed finish, and Ferran Torres added two more goals to seal a comprehensive win. Despite the hot and humid conditions, Barça maintained their pace and fluidity, with the second-half squad displaying energy and intent. FC Seoul showed resilience and managed a third goal late on, but were ultimately overwhelmed by Barça’s precision and tactical execution. This match not only highlighted the depth of Barcelona’s squad but also confirmed the readiness of several young and fringe players to contribute during the season. Yamal’s performance stood out as the most electrifying, underlining his potential as a future leader of the team. For FC Seoul, the match offered valuable exposure and moments of promise, particularly from Lingard and their high pressing in the first half. Overall, the game served as a successful showcase for Barcelona’s evolving squad under Flick, offering fans a glimpse of a team blending youth, experience, and attacking brilliance ahead of the upcoming campaign.
